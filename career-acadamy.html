Introduction

Poonam Mahajan is a prominent Indian politician and a member of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP). She represents the Mumbai North Central constituency in the Lok Sabha, the lower house of the Indian Parliament, since 2014. She is the daughter of late BJP leader and former Union Minister Pramod Mahajan, who was assassinated in 2006. She is also the sister of Rahul Mahajan, a former reality TV star and political activist. Mahajan has served as the National President of Bharatiya Janata Yuva Morcha (BJYM), the youth wing of the BJP, from December 2016 to September 2020. She is the first woman to hold this position. She is also the President of the Basketball Federation of India, the governing body of basketball in India, and the Chair of the Maharashtra State Animal Welfare Board, which oversees the welfare of animals in the state.

Political Career

  • 2006
    Poonam Mahajan joined politics as a primary member of the BJP at the age of 26.

  • 2009
    Appointed as the General Secretary for Bharatiya Janata Yuva Morcha (BJYM), Maharashtra unit.

  • 2010
    Appointed as the National Vice President of the Bharatiya Janata Yuva Morcha.

  • 2013
    Became the National Secretary of the BJP, focusing on strengthening the party organization

  • 2014
    Given a ticket for the Lok Sabha elections from Mumbai North Central, a Congress stronghold. She won, marking the BJP's first victory in that seat since independence.

  • 2016
    Took over as the National President of Bharatiya Janata Yuva Morcha (BJYM), the BJP's youth wing.

  • 2019
    Re-elected from Mumbai North Central, defeating her opponent Priya Dutt by a significant margin.
